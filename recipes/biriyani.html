This is a Biriyani Recipe

Description

Biryani is a beloved and flavorful rice dish that originates from the Indian subcontinent, with roots tracing back to Persian and Mughal influences. Known for its aromatic blend of spices, tender meats, or vegetables, biryani is a celebration of culinary artistry. The dish typically consists of basmati rice layered with marinated meat—such as chicken, lamb, or fish—or a mix of vegetables, along with a variety of spices like cardamom, cinnamon, and cloves. Slow-cooked to perfection, biryani is often garnished with fried onions, fresh coriander, and a touch of saffron for its signature golden hue, making it a feast for both the eyes and the palate.

Beyond its rich taste, biryani holds cultural significance and is a staple in festivities, weddings, and family gatherings. Each region in India offers its unique twist on biryani, such as the spicy Hyderabadi biryani, the coconut-infused Malabar biryani, or the fragrant Lucknowi (Awadhi) biryani.

Across the world, it has become a symbol of comfort food and shared moments, with variations influenced by local ingredients and tastes. Whether served with cooling raita, tangy pickles, or boiled eggs, biryani remains a timeless favorite that connects people through its rich flavors and history.

Ingredients

  • 150g basmati rice, rinsed
  • 600g boneless chicken thighs, cut into bite size pieces
  • 2 tbsp sunflower oil

Steps

  1. In a heavy based casserole pot heat the oil. Brown the chicken in the oil till the edges are golden. Remove to a plate.
  2. Lower the heat and add the onion and ginger, cook gently till soft but not coloured, add the spices and cook for another 3-4 minutes or until the spices become fragrant.
  3. Add the rice and the chicken and stir to coat both in the spices. Add the raisins and bay leaf. Pour in the stock and bring to the boil. Cover the pan and simmer for 18 minutes or until rice is cooked and the chicken is tender.
